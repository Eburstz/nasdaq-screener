# Understanding Signal Scores - Real Examples

## What Makes a High Score?

The scoring system (0-10) rewards **multiple confirmations** from different indicators. Here's how to read the scores.

---

- No confirmations (only 1 signal)
- Low ADX (<20) = choppy market
- Low volume (<1.0x) = no interest
- Extreme RSI (>80 or <20) without divergence
- High risk characteristics (micro cap + high volatility)

## 💡 Key Insights

### Multiple Confirmations = Higher Scores
A stock with **3 moderate signals** often scores higher than **1 strong signal alone**.

Example:
- **Stock A**: MA Cross (9/10) only = Score 2.7
- **Stock B**: MA Cross (6/10) + MACD + Volume 1.6x = Score 5.8

**Stock B is better** because of multiple confirmations.

### Volume is Critical
Without volume confirmation, even strong signals can fail.

Example:
- MA50×150 Bullish (9/10) + Volume 3.0x = Score 7-8
- MA50×150 Bullish (9/10) + Volume 0.8x = Score 3-4

The difference is **4-5 points** just from volume.

### ADX Filters Noise
Stocks in trending markets (ADX >25) are more reliable than choppy markets (ADX <20).

Example:
- MA Cross + ADX 40 = High confidence
- MA Cross + ADX 15 = Low confidence (skip)

### Risk Matters Too
Score tells you signal quality. Risk tells you position sizing.

Example:
- Score 8.0 + Low Risk = Full position
- Score 8.0 + High Risk = Reduced position or skip

## 🎓 Learning Exercise

When you review stocks in TOP PICKS, ask yourself:

1. **Why did it score this high?**
   → Check "Key Factors" column

2. **What signals are present?**
   → MA Cross? MACD? Volume? ADX?

3. **Are there 2+ confirmations?**
   → If yes = more reliable

4. **What's the risk level?**
   → Adjust position size accordingly

5. **Has it moved too much already?**
   → Check 5-Day % and 20-Day %

6. **Is it trending or choppy?**
   → ADX >25 = trending (good)
